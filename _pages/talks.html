--- permalink: /talks/ title: "Science" author_profile: true redirect_from: - /talks/ - /talks --- To this day, the fiducial Lambda Cold Dark Matter (LCDM) model still competes against alternative models that can potentially alleviate some tensions found with LCDM. A novel way to constrain dark matter models is possible through measuring the kinematic signatures of atomic hydrogen (HI) in galaxies. The 21-cm emission line of HI gas is broadened due to a galaxy's rotation. The extent of such broadening in a given galaxy is sensitive to the size, and hence mass of the dark matter halo hosting the gas. The use of HI gas a visible tracer for the dark matter distribution allows us to connect the dark universe to measurable quantities. [Brooks, Oman, & Frenk 2023](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2023MNRAS.522.4043B/abstract).
